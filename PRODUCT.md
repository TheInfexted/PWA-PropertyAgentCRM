# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Property agents in Malaysia, starting with one agent and a small team around them. They work a daily list of inbound and cold leads, usually at a desktop with a phone in hand, calling or messaging down the list. Today they track everything in a shared spreadsheet. The job to be done: contact each lead quickly, record what happened, and remember who to chase next, without losing anyone.

In team mode the workspace owner (or manager) sees every lead; individual agents see only the leads assigned to them.

## Product Purpose

A focused call-list CRM that replaces the spreadsheet. It cleans and de-duplicates phone numbers automatically, puts Call and WhatsApp one tap away, captures each outcome as a status plus a short note, and surfaces follow-ups that are due so leads stop going cold. It works for a solo agent (a workspace of one) and for a small team. Success looks like the agent reaching for this instead of the sheet, because it is genuinely faster and nothing falls through.

It is not a marketing site and not a sales-pipeline suite. The whole product is the daily worklist.

## Brand Personality

Warm and approachable, but unmistakably a work tool. It should feel like a helpful colleague, not a corporate control panel and not a hype landing page.

- Three words: warm, clear, dependable.
- Voice and tone: plain, friendly, encouraging. Short concrete sentences, no jargon, no buzzwords, no exclamation marks. It reassures rather than sells.
- Emotional goals: relief ("this is easier than my spreadsheet"), confidence (nothing slips), ease (it stays out of the way).

Warmth comes from softness, spacing, and human language, never from gimmicks, mascots, or decoration.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS template: cream-and-indigo palettes, rounded-card grids, three-equal-feature columns, the look that reads instantly as "AI generated".
- Cluttered enterprise CRM: Salesforce or Zoho style density, menus everywhere, intimidating panels of controls.
- Playful consumer app: bright bubbly colors, gamification, mascots, emoji-driven UI.
- Flashy marketing site: hero imagery, large animations, gradient washes, attention-grabbing motion. This is a tool, not a campaign.

## Design Principles

1. Faster than the sheet. Every screen serves the call, log, next loop. Never add a step the agent did not already have in Excel.
2. Warm, not cute. The product feels human and unintimidating through tone and softness, never through gimmicks or emoji.
3. Quiet under load. Agents grind long lists. The interface stays calm and legible at volume; nothing competes for attention except the next action.
4. One obvious move. On any screen, the primary action (call, WhatsApp, set status) is the clearest thing in view.
5. Trust through reliability. Clean numbers, no duplicates, nothing lost. The agent should never have to wonder whether the data is right.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Visible focus states on every control, full keyboard operation of the call list and forms, and contrast that holds up over long sessions. Honor prefers-reduced-motion. Status is never conveyed by color alone; it always carries a label or text.
